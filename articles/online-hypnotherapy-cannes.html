What the First Session Involves

The first session is 50 minutes and consists of detailed assessment. Christopher Murray asks you very specific questions about when the challenge started, what triggers it, how it's impacting your life functionally, and what you've already tried. He's not interviewing you for casual reasons. He's building a precise clinical picture. That information shapes the hypnotherapeutic approach entirely. Two people coming in with "anxiety" might need completely different interventions depending on the underlying pattern - one person's pattern might be anticipatory rumination, another's might be somatic panic. The assessment determines everything.

After assessment, you move into the first hypnotic induction. No theatrics. The space is quiet, you're lying down or sitting comfortably, and he guides your attention deliberately inward. The suggestions he introduces are calibrated to your specific context. You're not being told to "relax" generically. You're being guided into altered state and offered language that maps onto your actual neurobiology and the specific change you're pursuing. The session ends with clear, practical instructions for integration outside the session - because the real work happens when you're in actual situations, not in the therapy room.

Building Lasting Change

Most people see measurable shifts within 2-4 sessions. Some take longer depending on how entrenched the pattern is. The work continues between sessions. Christopher Murray gives you specific practices, reframes, and attention anchors you deploy when you're triggered in real life. That's where the actual learning happens. The hypnotherapy creates the conditions for change. Your application of that change in actual situations cements it. This is why hypnotherapy for confidence works best with active engagement, not passive hope that shifting in the therapy room will somehow transfer to real life without intention.

A typical course runs 6-12 sessions spaced two weeks apart. You complete assessment, establish the intervention, reinforce with repetition, then taper off. By the end, the new pattern has become your baseline. You're not "managing" the old problem anymore. It's genuinely resolved. Your first session establishes the foundation, and each subsequent session builds on that work. Most people describe the shift as a combination of relief and quiet surprise that something they thought was permanent could move so quickly.
