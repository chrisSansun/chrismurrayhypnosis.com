How Cognitive Hypnotherapy Works

Cognitive hypnotherapy combines neuroscience-backed hypnosis with cognitive psychology. It's targeted, practical, and rooted in evidence. The approach identifies the automatic thought patterns driving your issue, then uses hypnosis to restructure them while your mind is most receptive to change.

In the first session, you'll talk through what's brought you in - perhaps anxiety, confidence gaps, sleep issues, or patterns you want to shift. We map the mechanism: what thoughts trigger the feeling, what beliefs underlie those thoughts, what body response follows. Then in hypnosis, we work with those patterns when your mind is most open to change. You're never out of control. You hear everything. You guide the process.

The work sticks because it addresses causes, not just symptoms. Anxiety before a presentation isn't the problem - the belief about what the presentation means is. When you shift that belief, the anxiety has no fuel left.

Research and Evidence

Cognitive hypnotherapy is backed by neuroscience and clinical psychology. Hypnosis isn't sleep or loss of control - it's a state of focused attention where the mind becomes more receptive to change. Functional MRI studies show that hypnotic states activate the prefrontal cortex (thinking brain) differently, making new thought patterns easier to establish and hold.

The cognitive part addresses the actual content - the thoughts and beliefs driving the issue. By combining both, you get durability. The changes aren't just relaxation - they're restructuring how your mind approaches stress, confidence, and challenge.

Multiple clinical trials have shown hypnotherapy for anxiety is as effective as cognitive-behavioural therapy and significantly faster for many people. Video delivery doesn't reduce effectiveness - in fact, research suggests clients may engage more fully when working from a space they feel safe in.
