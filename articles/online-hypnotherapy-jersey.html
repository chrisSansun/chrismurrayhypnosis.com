The mechanics are straightforward. You'll meet with me over a secure video call, just as you would with any therapist. What Happens During Your First Session

The first hour is assessment and history-taking, not treatment. I need to understand the actual architecture of your problem. Not just "I have anxiety" but what specifically triggers it, how your body responds, what you've already tried, and why you're reaching out now. I'll ask about your sleep, your physical health, your stress levels, your relationships, and your work. This matters because hypnotherapy doesn't work in a vacuum. It works within the context of your whole life.

Only after I understand the problem do I introduce hypnotic technique. We'll agree on what change looks like, set realistic expectations, and build a framework for how we'll work together. Most people need between four and eight sessions to see meaningful results, though that varies. Some things shift quickly. Deeper patterns take time. I'm not here to promise overnight transformation. I'm here to do work that actually holds up when you leave the session and go back to your real life.

The Science Behind Online Hypnotherapy

Cognitive hypnotherapy draws on neuroplasticity research. Essentially, your brain rewires itself based on where you direct attention and what you repeatedly practice thinking. When you're hypnotized, the prefrontal cortex - the thinking, evaluating part of your brain - quiets down slightly. That makes space for the limbic system, which processes emotion and memory, to update. Research from the NIH has shown that clinical hypnotherapy produces measurable changes in brain activation patterns and can be as effective as cognitive behavioral therapy for conditions like anxiety and insomnia, without the six-month waiting list.

Being online doesn't change that neuroscience. Your brain doesn't know whether you're sitting in a clinic or at your kitchen table. What matters is your attention, your willingness to engage, and the skill of the practitioner guiding the process.
