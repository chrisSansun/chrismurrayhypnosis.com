Online Hypnotherapy in Dublin

Dublin professionals often tell me they've been holding tension in their shoulders for years, or that sleep disappeared when the promotion came through. They're usually surprised to hear that I'm not in Dublin - I'm based in Sri Lanka and run all my sessions online. It doesn't change what's possible. Online hypnotherapy in Dublin works exactly the same way as in-person work because the therapeutic shift happens inside you, not in a room. What matters is the quality of your attention and the practitioner's skill in helping you access it.

Why Online Hypnotherapy Works for Dublin Clients

The technical argument is straightforward. Hypnosis doesn't require being in the same physical space. It's a state of focused attention where your unconscious mind becomes more receptive to suggestion and reframing. That state emerges through guided conversation and specific language patterns, not through proximity. Video calls deliver this just as effectively as face-to-face work, and sometimes more effectively because you're in your own familiar environment.

For Dublin-based clients, online work removes friction. You don't need to find a practitioner with availability that matches your schedule, negotiate Dublin traffic, or block out an afternoon in your calendar. You show up to the call from your home or office. Many of my Dublin clients tell me they actually prefer this - they feel more grounded and resourceful in their own space than they would sitting in an unfamiliar therapy room.

The research backs this up. Clinical outcomes for cognitive hypnotherapy don't show meaningful differences between online and in-person delivery when the practitioner is properly trained. What matters is the practitioner's ability to build rapport, assess what's really driving the problem, and design an intervention that fits your specific situation.

What Brings Dublin Clients to Hypnotherapy

I work with a lot of high-achieving professionals in Dublin. Executives, business owners, people managing teams and complex problems. They typically come because something that used to work stopped working. Anxiety that disrupts sleep or focus is common - the mind's threat-detection system has gotten too sensitive. Executive burnout is another pattern. And ADHD, which I specialize in, shows up regularly in people who've been compensating through sheer willpower until they can't anymore.

The other big bucket is performance. People who want to speak with more confidence, make better decisions under pressure, or stop second-guessing themselves in meetings. Hypnotherapy for executives works particularly well here because we're not just managing symptoms - we're shifting the underlying patterns of how you process pressure and make decisions.

What I notice across all these presentations is that Dublin's pace amplifies everything. The city's intensity, the commute, the social expectations, the professional demands - they all stack up. Online hypnotherapy gives you a tool to reset that load, without needing to leave your environment to do it.

How Online Sessions Actually Work

Your first session runs for ninety minutes. We spend most of that time talking - I'm listening for patterns, understanding what's driving the problem, and assessing whether hypnotherapy's the right fit for what you need. I ask direct questions about how the anxiety shows up, when it started, what you've already tried. Then, if we're both confident it's the right approach, I'll guide you into hypnosis during that first session and do some foundational work.

Subsequent sessions are usually sixty minutes. You'll typically have between three and six sessions depending on what we're working with. The work is collaborative - I design specific suggestions and reframing based on what emerged in our conversation, then guide you into that resourceful state and deliver it. You're not asleep. You're completely aware, entirely in control, and you'll remember everything.

We use secure video conferencing. I recommend finding a quiet space, maybe somewhere you feel comfortable and safe. Some clients prefer their bedroom, others use a home office. The practitioner matters more than the setting. What you need is my full attention and yours - no distractions, no performance, just focused work on what needs to shift.
