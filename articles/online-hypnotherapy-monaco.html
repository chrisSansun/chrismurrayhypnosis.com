How Online Hypnotherapy Actually Works

A lot of people have ideas about what hypnotherapy is. Stage hypnosis has done a lot of damage to the word. Most of it's wrong. Real hypnotherapy, the clinical kind, isn't about swinging watches or making you cluck like a chicken. It's focused work on how you think. It's applied neuroscience dressed up in a language your conscious mind can actually use.

Here's what happens. In a session, you talk first, just like regular therapy. You tell me what's happening. Sleep problems. Anxiety. A pattern you keep repeating. Then we move into the hypnotic part. You stay fully conscious. You can hear everything I say. You're not asleep. What changes is your level of suggestibility. That's a technical term in cognitive psychology. It means your critical filter relaxes a bit, and direct suggestion becomes more effective. Your brain's filter is running constantly, rejecting ideas that don't match your existing beliefs. In hypnosis, that filter eases, so we can introduce new ways of thinking more directly.

Online doesn't reduce effectiveness. Research from the American Society of Clinical Hypnosis confirms that remote hypnotherapy produces the same clinical outcomes as face-to-face work. The mechanism is the same. Your nervous system responds the same way to a guided session whether I'm in the room or on video. You get the same access to that more suggestible state. The only real difference is comfort. You're in your own space. You're not traveling. That's often better.

What that means in practice: I understand neuroscience. I understand ethics. I understand the difference between what works and what sounds good. I won't overpromise. I won't treat something outside my scope. I won't use hypnotherapy as a replacement for medical care when medical care is what you need.

Always discuss any new treatment with your doctor.

Monaco's professional culture respects credentials. That's good. It means when we work together, you know I've met actual standards, not just paid for a certificate online. It also means I'm committed to not wasting your time. You're busy. You're expensive. Our work together is efficient.

What to Expect in Your First Session

We do a free 30-minute consultation first. That's not a sales call. I'm genuinely figuring out whether I can help you and whether we're a good fit. I ask about what's been happening, how long it's been happening, what you've already tried. I explain how hypnotherapy specifically works, in language that makes sense, not mystique. Then I ask what you'd want to be different. That clarity matters. "Feel better" isn't a goal. "Sleep through the night without waking at 2 a.m. from racing thoughts" is.

If we move forward, the first paid session is 75 minutes. We do intake properly. We talk about your history, your patterns, what might be driving what you're experiencing. Then we do hypnotherapy. You'll experience that shift I mentioned, that easing of the critical filter. It's not dramatic. It's subtle. Slightly heavier, slightly more relaxed, slightly quieter in your head. We might work with suggestion around sleep, or anxiety, or reframing perfectionism. I might teach you self-hypnosis. By the end, you're alert, grounded, often surprised at how straightforward it was.

Subsequent sessions are typically 60 minutes. We open with feedback from how you've been since we last met. Then we do hypnotherapy with increasingly targeted work. Most people need 4-6 sessions. Some need more. Some need fewer. It depends on what we're treating and how your mind responds to suggestion.

Finding the Right Therapist for You

Monaco's internet is full of hypnotherapists now. Some are excellent. Some are not. Some have real training. Some have a weekend certification. If you're going to do this, do it properly. Ask about credentials. Ask about supervised clinical training. Ask about continuing education. Ask about ethics registration. A good therapist will answer all of that without defensiveness.

You should also feel something when you talk to them. Not love, not best friends. But trust. Can you imagine telling this person your real struggles without performing? Can you imagine sitting quietly with them while your mind processes something? That matters. It's also something you can only figure out in conversation.

High-performers specifically often benefit from someone who understands achievement culture, who doesn't pathologise ambition, who gets that you're not anxious because you're weak.
